The Biggest Failures of Gaming Companies

w

Software failures in the gaming industry can have a significant impact on the user experience and engagement. Let's explore some common types of software failures related to gaming:

The Transient Tricksters:

These are fleeting glitches triggered by specific conditions. For example, a web-based game that displays incorrectly only on a specific browser version.

The Permanent Party Crashers:

Permanent failures are the uninvited guests that crash consistently, usually pointing to a bug in the code that needs a patch or update. A software update gone wrong, causing a persistent issue until a fix is rolled out, is a common example.

The Intermittent Imps:

Intermittent failures are the most frustrating as they come and go, making them hard to reproduce and diagnose. These gremlins can be caused by hardware issues, network problems, or even cosmic rays.

The Corrupting Culprits vs. The Clean Mistakes:

Data-corruption failures modify or destroy the information the game handles, leading to lost progress or corrupted save files. Non-corruption failures cause the game to malfunction but leave the data untouched, like a game freezing but not affecting the save data.

Software failures in gaming can lead to user frustration, dissatisfaction, and disengagement, affecting the overall experience and success of a game.

1. EA's "Battlefront II" Loot Box Controversy

In 2017, Electronic Arts (EA) faced widespread backlash for the implementation of loot boxes in their highly anticipated game, "Star Wars Battlefront II." Players criticized the system as being a form of gambling, as loot boxes offered a random chance of obtaining powerful items or character upgrades, giving an unfair advantage to those willing to spend more money.

2. Ubisoft's Server Crashes

Ubisoft, a major game developer known for games like Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six series, is infamous for their server crashes. Every now and then, there will be server crashes or unannounced maintenance, which is very annoying as it is a live server-based game with a ranking system.

These failures serve as cautionary tales for the gaming industry, highlighting the importance of listening to their player base, prioritizing game quality, and making ethical business decisions.
